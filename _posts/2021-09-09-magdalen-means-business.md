It was gratifying to be recognised by Magdalen College for the work done and contribution to Open Source and Humanitarian Software.

The exhibit — part of Magdalen's *Magdalen Means Business* showcase — highlights the [Power of Software](https://magdalenmeansbusiness.magd.ox.ac.uk/power-of-software/) and profiles alumni whose work in software has had meaningful real-world impact.

For me, the recognition connects two things that have always felt deeply linked: the rigour and values instilled at Oxford, and the open source and humanitarian work that followed. The Sahana Disaster Management System — which began as an emergency response to the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami — is perhaps the clearest expression of what software *can* do when built openly, by a community, for people who need it most.

The open source principles that underpin that work — transparency, community governance, the freedom to inspect and improve — are not just technical choices. They are ethical ones.
