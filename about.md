## Chamindra de Silva

Chamindra de Silva is an award-winning Innovation Director and Product Engineering Expert with 20+ years of multi-sector experience across Banking, FinTech, Insurance, ISV, Government, and INGO. He is passionate about incubating high-energy, globally distributed teams to create great products using Lean Startup and Agile methodologies.

He has built four internationally award-winning products in Open Banking, Open Innovation, Education, and Humanitarian Response, with recognition from Gartner, MIT, UNDP, the Free Software Foundation, the Open Source Initiative (OSI), and the World Information Technology and Services Alliance (WITSA).

## Faith & Values

Chamindra is a [Christian](https://www.st-helens.org.uk/), and his faith shapes how he approaches his work — as a vocation rather than a career, oriented toward the long-term flourishing of people and communities rather than short-term commercial gain. This is most visible in his sustained commitment to humanitarian open source, sustainability, and community-governed technology: work that is difficult, slow, and often unrewarded by conventional metrics, but worth doing.

He believes technology serves best when it is accountable, transparent, and governed by people who care about its effects on those least able to protect themselves from its failures.

## AI Governance

Chamindra is a **Maintainer of the [FINOS AI Governance Framework](https://github.com/finos/ai-governance-framework)** (AIGF) — an open-source, community-developed framework for governing the deployment of AI systems in financial services, hosted by the Fintech Open Source Foundation. He works alongside maintainers from Citi, Morgan Stanley, NatWest, and Scott Logic to build practical, operational governance processes that financial institutions can actually use.

His contributions to the AIGF span three areas:

**Use-Case Taxonomy & Regulatory Alignment.** He has developed a classification approach that maps AI use cases to their SR 11-7 / SR 26-2 (Federal Reserve model risk management) governance requirements — answering the practical question every bank must answer before deployment: does this system require full MRM treatment, and at what validation tier? The taxonomy introduces four classification axes (SR 11-7 applicability, decision influence mode, materiality tier, and validation tier) to enable consistent, defensible governance decisions.

**ROI & Adoption.** He is developing a structured ROI model that translates AIGF adoption into measurable business value — reduced approval cycle times for low-risk use cases, lower incident remediation costs, improved regulatory auditability — making it possible for AI leadership and CTO offices to build the internal case for structured governance.

**AIGF MCP Server.** He has proposed and is championing an [MCP server](https://github.com/finos/ai-governance-framework/issues/286) for the AIGF that uses agent-assisted workflows to generate a customised governance framework — one that respects an institution's existing risk infrastructure, policies, and approval processes rather than replacing them wholesale. The agentic output is designed for human review and adaptation, not autonomous action.

As an AI-native Solutions Architect and Certified Technical Product Owner, Chamindra explores how open source principles apply to the governance of foundation models, LLMs, and AI infrastructure. He advocates for transparency in training data, open weights, and OSI-compliant licensing as the baseline for trustworthy AI.

## InnerSource

As [FINOS InnerSource SIG Lead](https://github.com/finos/InnerSource) and DevOps Product Owner & InnerSource Lead at Citi UK, Chamindra works to bring open source culture — contribution norms, transparent governance, meritocratic participation — inside large enterprise environments. Financial institutions face unique challenges of code duplication and organisational silos that InnerSource directly addresses.

## Open Source & HFOSS

Chamindra is the concept founder of **HFOSS** (Humanitarian Free and Open Source Software) — the application of FOSS principles to coordination problems in humanitarian and disaster-response domains. He was project lead of the [Sahana Disaster Management System](https://sahanafoundation.org/about/) from its inception in Sri Lanka following the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami, and continues as Director of the Sahana Foundation, one of the most deployed open source disaster management tools in the world.

He is a Charter Member of the [Open Source Initiative (OSI)](https://opensource.org/), a recipient of the [Free Software Foundation Social Benefit Award (2006)](https://www.fsf.org/news/social-benefit-award-2006), and contributes to FOSS communities including Apache, OLPC, UNDP IOSN, and AsiaOSS. He co-founded a [W3C Working Group on Emergency Interoperability (EIIF)](https://www.w3.org/2005/Incubator/eiif/) and is a member of IEEE-CS and ISSA.
